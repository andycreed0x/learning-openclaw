Your personal, self-hosted AI assistant platform

Welcome to Session 1 of the Learning OpenClaw crash course. In this session we will cover what OpenClaw is, the problem it solves, and give you a high-level understanding of how it works.

The Problem

  • AI assistants are locked in silos
  • No way to self-host your own assistant
  • No multi-channel support — stuck in one app
  • Vendor lock-in to a single AI provider
  • Your data lives on someone else's servers
 You today:

Today, if you want to use an AI assistant, you are tied to a single vendor. Your conversations, your data, your context — all trapped in their platform. You cannot choose which model to use per conversation, and you certainly cannot reach your assistant from WhatsApp, Telegram, and Discord simultaneously.

The Solution: OpenClaw

A personal, self-hosted AI assistant platform

Local-first
Runs on your hardware. Your data stays yours.
Multi-channel
WhatsApp, Telegram, Discord, Slack, Web — all at once.
Model-agnostic
Use OpenAI, Anthropic, Gemini, Ollama, or any compatible API.
Open Source

OpenClaw is the answer. It is a self-hosted platform that lets you run your own AI assistant. It supports multiple messaging channels simultaneously, works with any AI model provider, and is fully open source under the AGPLv3 license. Your data never leaves your infrastructure.

High-Level Architecture

graph LR U["👤 User"] --> CH["📱 Channels"] CH --> GW["🔀 Gateway"] GW --> AI["🤖 AI Models"] AI --> T["🔧 Tools"]
4 core layers: Users interact through Channels, which connect to the Gateway, which routes to AI Models that can invoke Tools.
At its simplest, OpenClaw has four layers. Users send messages through channels like WhatsApp or Telegram. Those messages flow into the Gateway, which is the central orchestrator. The Gateway routes them to AI agents, and those agents can call tools to browse the web, execute code, schedule tasks, and more.

How It Works

A simplified message flow

  💬 Message                📨 Response
     |                        ^
     v                        |
 +----------+           +----------+
 | Channel  |           | Channel  |
 | (WA/TG)  |           | (WA/TG)  |
 +----+-----+           +----+-----+
      |                       ^
      v     WebSocket         |
 +----------+           +----------+
 | Gateway  | --------> | Gateway  |
 | (Router) |           | (Router) |
 +----+-----+           +----+-----+
      |                       ^
      v                       |
 +----------+           +----------+
 |  Agent   | --------> |  Agent   |
 | (AI+Sys) |  thinks   | (AI+Sys) |
 +----+-----+           +----+-----+
      |                       ^
      v                       |
 +----------+           +----------+
 |  Tools   | --------> |  Tools   |
 | (opt.)   |  results  | (opt.)   |
 +----------+           +----------+
Here is the message lifecycle. A user sends a message through a channel. The channel forwards it to the Gateway via WebSocket. The Gateway routes it to the appropriate agent, which processes it using an AI model. If the agent needs to take action, it calls tools. The response flows back through the same path to the user.

OpenClaw is truly model-agnostic. You can use OpenAI, Anthropic Claude, Google Gemini, or run models locally with Ollama for complete privacy. OpenRouter gives you access to over 100 models. And if you have any OpenAI-compatible API server, OpenClaw works with it out of the box. You can even use different models for different agents.
